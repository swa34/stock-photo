02 · Where the files live

Three storage jobs, three different tools

“Cloud storage” is not one thing. The mistake that gets expensive is using a sync app (built for documents) to hold terabytes of photos. Each job below wants a different kind of service.

The three jobs storage has to do for a photo archive. Prices are list rates as of June 2026.

Object storage
the permanent RAW archive		 Backblaze B2, Cloudflare R2, AWS S3 ~$1–23 / TB / mo
(see table below)		 Effectively unlimited, priced by the terabyte, keeps your laptop empty. Catch: no “drag-and-drop folder” — you push/pull files with a sync tool or app.

Object storage — the part to understand for cost

This is where the master archive should live, and where the real money decisions are. The headline price is per terabyte, but the hidden cost is egress — the fee to download your own files back out. It is the single biggest trap in cloud storage.

Worked example · 15 TB on Backblaze B2

Storage: 15 TB × $6.95 = ≈ $104/mo.

Free downloads: the allowance is 3× what you store = 45 TB every month. A medium month — pulling back a few TB of files (say 5 TB) — sits well inside that, so egress is $0.

All-in ≈ $104/mo. The ~$10/TB rate only starts once downloads pass 45 TB in a single month — e.g. a heavy 50 TB month adds just 5 × $10 = $50.

takeaway

Backblaze B2 (cheap, generous free downloads) , You can always switch to Cloudflare R2 if you start to see a large amount of pull files (downloads are always free). Use for Glacier Deep Archive only for a deep-freeze copy you hope to never open.

04 · The storefront

Three ways to actually sell the photos

Every storefront has to do the same three things: show the gallery, let people search, and take payment then deliver the file. The options differ in how much is bundled for you versus assembled by you — which is the trade between cost, control, and effort.

Each diagram below is a stack: the same rows every time, so you can see at a glance which pieces come bundled and which are external (a separate service with its own bill). Reminder of the colours: tool you run storage storefront / checkout.

06 · A nudge toward a choice

Which one is probably right

Not advice to follow blindly — just the most common fit for each situation, to give your own research a starting point.

If you want…
To start selling now

…and never think about servers, pick a managed platform (Option A). SmugMug or PhotoShelter for unlimited JPEG storage and built-in checkout.

≈ $10–45 / month, all-in
If you want…
Lowest cost at scale

…and you’re comfortable maintaining a site, self-host WordPress (Option B) with Easy Digital Downloads and images offloaded to Backblaze B2.

≈ $10–20 / month + $7 per TB
If you want…
A one-of-a-kind site

…that no existing platform can produce totally custom (Option C) in Python or Node. Most control, most work.

≈ $15–60 / month infra + build time
